MELANOMA


Definition:

Melanoma is a type of skin cancer that develops in the melanocytes, the pigment-producing cells in the skin. It is the most serious form of skin cancer because it can spread to other parts of the body if not detected and treated early. Here is some basic information about melanoma and its common symptoms:

Cause:

Several factors contribute to the development of melanoma:

  • Exposure to Ultraviolet (UV) Radiation: From the sun or artificial sources like tanning beds.
  • Skin Type: Fair-skinned individuals with light-colored eyes and a history of sunburns are at higher risk.
  • Family History: Having a family member with melanoma increases the risk.
  • Moles: Presence of a large number of moles or atypical moles increases risk.
  • Weakened Immune System: Conditions like HIV or organ transplantation can increase risk.

Common Symptoms:

Common signs of melanoma include:

  • Change in a Mole: Size, shape, color, or elevation changes in existing moles.
  • Asymmetry: Melanomas are often asymmetrical.
  • Irregular Borders: Borders may be irregular or poorly defined.
  • Varied Colors: Melanomas can have multiple colors within the same lesion.
  • Diameter: Melanomas are often larger than 6 millimeters.
  • Evolving or Changing: Any mole or skin lesion that changes over time should be evaluated.
  • Pain or Sensation: Some melanomas may cause pain or unusual sensations.
  • Spreading: Melanoma can spread to nearby lymph nodes or other organs.
  • Nail Changes: Subungual melanoma can cause dark lines or streaks under a nail.
