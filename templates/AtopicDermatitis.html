ATOPIC DERMATITIS

Definition:

Atopic dermatitis is a long-term skin condition causing itchy, inflamed skin. It's a type of eczema often starting in childhood but affecting people of all ages.

Cause:

The exact cause of atopic dermatitis isn't clear, but genetics, environment, and immune system play roles. A family history of atopic diseases increases risk.

Common Symptoms of Atopic Dermatitis:

  • Itching (Pruritus): Intense itching is the main symptom. Scratching can worsen irritation.
  • Redness and Inflammation: Affected skin becomes red, swollen, and inflamed, with varying severity.
  • Dryness: Skin becomes extremely dry, prone to cracking, scaling, and peeling.
  • Rash: A common feature, appearing as raised, scaly patches, bumps, or small fluid-filled blisters.
  • Oozing and Crusting: Severe cases may lead to oozing fluid and crust formation, especially with repeated scratching.
  • Skin Thickening: Chronic scratching or rubbing can thicken the skin, known as lichenification.
  • Skin Discoloration: Some individuals may notice patches of lighter or darker skin.
  • Secondary Infections: Scratching breaks the skin's barrier, making it prone to bacterial or viral infections.
